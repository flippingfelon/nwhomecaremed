Why Ceiling Lifts Are a Game-Changer for Home Care

Manual patient transfers are the leading cause of back injuries among home caregivers. A ceiling track lift eliminates the physical strain entirely — a motorized unit does the lifting while the caregiver simply guides the sling. This means safer transfers, more dignity for the person being moved, and dramatically lower injury risk for family members and professional aides.

Unlike portable floor lifts, ceiling-mounted systems take up zero floor space, require no maneuvering around furniture, and can span multiple rooms on a single rail — making them ideal for aging in place at home.

Ceiling Lift FAQ

What is a ceiling track lift?
A ceiling track lift is a motorized hoist mounted on a rail system attached to your ceiling. It uses a sling to safely lift and transfer a person between bed, wheelchair, bath, toilet, and other locations — completely eliminating manual lifting by caregivers.
Can ceiling lifts go from room to room?
Yes. Molift rail systems support straight runs and curved sections (30°, 45°, 60°, and 90° turns), allowing transfers through doorways and between multiple rooms on a single continuous track.
How much weight can a Molift ceiling lift support?
The Molift Air 200 supports up to 451 lbs (205 kg) and the Molift Air 300 supports up to 660 lbs (300 kg). We'll recommend the right model based on your needs.
Will the rail damage my ceiling?
No. The rail system is securely mounted to ceiling joists or structural beams. Once installed, it looks like a clean, low-profile track — and it can be removed later without major structural damage if needed.
How much does a ceiling lift system cost?
A basic single-room straight track with hoist typically ranges from $3,000–$6,000. Multi-room systems with curved rails can range from $6,000–$12,000+. We provide free assessments with detailed written estimates.
