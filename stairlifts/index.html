Keep Using Every Floor of Your Home

Stairs don't have to mean giving up your bedroom, your laundry room, or your independence. A stairlift lets you move between floors safely and comfortably — and it's one of the most impactful aging in place investments you can make.

Rather than waiting for a fall or a crisis, experts recommend installing a stairlift proactively when you first notice stairs becoming tiring, when you grip the railing more tightly than before, or when you start avoiding certain floors. Getting ahead of the problem keeps you safe and gives you time to adapt on your terms.

Harmar Stairlift Features

Ultra-Compact Folded Profile

Folds to just 10.5 inches from the wall when not in use — other household members can walk the stairs normally.

300–600 lb Capacity

Models available for a wide range of body types. The seat swivels at top and bottom for safe entry and exit.

Battery Backup (60 Trips)

Runs on continuously charging batteries — up to 60 trips during a power outage. You'll never be stranded.

The rail attaches to stair treads — no wall damage, no structural concerns, and easier removal if needed.

Stairlift FAQ

How wide does a staircase need to be for a stairlift?
Most Harmar stairlifts require a minimum staircase width of about 26–28 inches. When folded, the unit protrudes only 10.5 inches from the wall, leaving plenty of room for other people to walk the stairs.
What happens during a power outage?
Harmar stairlifts run on battery power that charges continuously from a standard household outlet. The battery backup provides up to 60 full trips during a power outage, so you'll never be stranded on the staircase.
Is the stairlift attached to the wall or the stairs?
The rail mounts directly to the stair treads — not the wall. This means no wall damage and a cleaner installation. Rail brackets only need to be attached to select stairs, not every step.
Can a stairlift handle curved stairs?
NW Homecare Medical specializes in straight-run Harmar stairlifts. If your staircase has curves or intermediate landings, contact us — we can discuss options or refer you to a curved stairlift specialist.
How much does a stairlift cost?
Harmar straight stairlifts typically range from $3,000 to $6,000 including installation, depending on staircase length and selected features. We provide free in-home assessments with written estimates.
When should I install a stairlift?
Experts recommend acting proactively — when stairs start feeling more tiring, when you grip the railing tightly, or when you begin avoiding certain floors. Installing before a crisis gives you time to adapt comfortably rather than making a rushed decision after a fall.
