The Bathroom Is the Most Dangerous Room in the Home

According to the CDC, more than 230,000 Americans visit the emergency room each year due to bathroom injuries — and 80% of those involve the bathtub or shower. Slippery surfaces, high tub walls, and narrow entries create fall hazards that get worse as mobility changes with age.

An accessible bathroom remodel eliminates these risks with zero-threshold entries, slip-resistant surfaces, built-in grab bars, and fold-down shower seats — so you can bathe safely and independently.

Bath Remodel FAQ

How long does an accessible bathroom remodel take?
Most Best Bath walk-in shower installations are completed in one day. A more complex full-bathroom remodel with plumbing changes may take 2–3 days. We'll give you a clear timeline during your assessment.
What is a barrier-free shower?
A barrier-free (or zero-threshold) shower has no step or curb to step over. This eliminates the #1 bathroom fall hazard and allows wheelchair or walker access directly into the shower area.
Does Best Bath offer a warranty?
Yes. Best Bath offers a 30-year warranty on their composite wall construction. As a certified dealer, NW Homecare Medical ensures your installation is fully backed by the manufacturer.
Can you remodel my existing shower without a full gut renovation?
In most cases, yes. Best Bath's multi-piece prefabricated systems are designed to retrofit into existing bathroom spaces without tearing down walls. We fit the new system within your current footprint.
How much does an accessible bathroom remodel cost?
A typical Best Bath walk-in shower retrofit ranges from $5,000 to $15,000 depending on size, features, and site conditions. We provide free written estimates after an in-home assessment.
