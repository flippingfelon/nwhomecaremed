Frequently Asked Questions

What does a Certified Aging In Place Specialist (CAPS) do?
A CAPS professional assesses your home for safety risks and recommends modifications — grab bars, walk-in showers, ramps, ceiling lifts, stairlifts — that let you live independently as you age. NW Homecare Medical holds all three CAPS certifications (I, II, and III) from the National Association of Home Builders.
How much do aging in place modifications cost?
Costs vary by project scope. Grab bars start around $200–$400. Ramps run $2,000–$8,000+. Stairlifts and ceiling lifts fall in the $3,000–$10,000 range. Bathroom remodels range from $5,000–$15,000+. We provide free in-home assessments with written estimates — no pressure, no obligation.
Does Medicare cover these modifications?
Medicare generally doesn't cover structural home modifications, but certain items like ceiling lifts may qualify as Durable Medical Equipment. Oregon Medicaid waivers, VA benefits, and long-term care insurance may also provide funding. We can help you understand your options.
