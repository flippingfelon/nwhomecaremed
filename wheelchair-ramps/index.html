Safe Entry and Exit — the Foundation of Aging In Place

If you can't get in and out of your own home safely, nothing else matters. Steps and uneven thresholds are one of the most common barriers to independent living for people with reduced mobility, wheelchair users, and those recovering from surgery.

A properly installed modular ramp turns your front door from an obstacle into an accessible, code-compliant entry point — and because EZ Access ramps are bolt-together aluminum, they can be reconfigured or relocated if your needs change.

Wheelchair Ramp FAQ

How long does a wheelchair ramp need to be?
ADA guidelines recommend a 1:12 slope ratio — meaning 1 foot of ramp length for every inch of rise. A home with a 24-inch entry step would need approximately 24 feet of ramp run, which can be configured in an L-shape or switchback to fit the available space.
Do modular ramps require a building permit?
Requirements vary by municipality. In many Oregon jurisdictions, modular ramp systems that don't permanently attach to the structure may be exempt from permits. We research permit requirements for your location and handle applications as part of our service.
Can a modular ramp be relocated?
Yes. EZ Access PATHWAY 3G ramps are bolt-together systems that can be fully disassembled and reconfigured at a new location. This makes them ideal for renters, temporary recovery needs, or if you move to a different home.
How much does a wheelchair ramp cost?
EZ Access PATHWAY 3G ramp systems typically range from $2,000 for a short straight run to $8,000+ for longer configurations with platforms and turns. Pricing depends on total length, number of platforms, and handrail style. We provide free site assessments with written estimates.
Will a ramp work on uneven ground?
Yes. The PATHWAY 3G system uses adjustable legs and concrete pad footings that accommodate uneven terrain. We assess your specific ground conditions and engineer a stable, level installation.
